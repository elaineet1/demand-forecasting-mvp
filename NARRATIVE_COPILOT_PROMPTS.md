# Narrative Co-Pilot Prompt Guide

Use these prompts in the **Optional Focus Note** field on the Narrative Co-Pilot page.

The best prompts are:
- specific about audience
- clear about the decision or risk to emphasize
- short enough to keep the narrative focused

---

## Executive Summary Prompts

1. Summarize this forecast run for senior management in plain English, focusing on demand outlook, stock risk, and recommended actions.
2. Write a board-style summary highlighting the most important inventory risks and the biggest reorder priorities.
3. Explain the overall forecast in a way a non-technical executive can understand.
4. Focus on the top business actions leadership should take this month based on this forecast.
5. Summarize whether current stock levels appear healthy, risky, or excessive across the assortment.
6. Highlight the biggest risks to sales if understock items are not addressed soon.
7. Explain whether the forecast is reliable enough for planning, including any confidence caveats.
8. Write a concise executive update on projected demand, stock exposure, and key planning implications.
9. Focus on the most urgent decisions management should review before approving purchases.
10. Turn this forecast run into a 5-bullet leadership summary for a meeting.

---

## Buyer Brief Prompts

1. Write a buyer brief focused on which SKUs need immediate reorder attention.
2. Summarize the top replenishment priorities for this forecast run.
3. Focus on the items with the largest reorder quantities and explain why they matter.
4. Write a planning note for the purchasing team highlighting urgent buy decisions.
5. Summarize which brands need the fastest action and what the likely reason is.
6. Focus on understock-risk SKUs that could affect near-term sales.
7. Write a replenishment summary that separates urgent actions from routine actions.
8. Explain which items should be manually reviewed before placing purchase orders.
9. Focus on buyer actions needed over the next 3 months based on projected demand.
10. Write a concise trading and replenishment brief for a weekly planning meeting.

---

## Risk Summary Prompts

1. Write a risk summary focused on understock and overstock exposure.
2. Explain the main inventory risks if no action is taken this month.
3. Focus on the biggest understock threats to revenue and availability.
4. Focus on the biggest overstock risks and where clearance or markdowns may be needed.
5. Highlight where the forecast relies heavily on fallback logic and explain the planning risk.
6. Summarize the main data quality issues that could reduce forecast confidence.
7. Explain where forecast uncertainty is highest and what that means for decisions.
8. Write a risk memo for management covering stock imbalance and model reliability.
9. Focus on SKUs or brands that deserve manual review because they carry unusual risk.
10. Summarize the operational risks hidden in this forecast run, including stock, data, and model limitations.

---

## Brand-Specific Prompts

1. Summarize the planning situation for this brand only.
2. Explain the main reorder risks for this brand in buyer-friendly language.
3. Focus on whether this brand appears overstocked or understocked overall.
4. Write a short planning note for a vendor review meeting for this brand.
5. Highlight the most urgent SKUs within this brand and explain why they stand out.
6. Explain whether this brand's forecast appears reliable or heavily dependent on fallback rules.
7. Focus on the 3-month demand outlook for this brand and the stock implications.
8. Write a summary of the buying actions needed for this brand right now.
9. Explain whether current stock for this brand looks balanced against demand.
10. Summarize the risks and opportunities for this brand in a concise management note.

---

## Presentation And Email Prompts

1. Rewrite this as a short email to the purchasing manager.
2. Turn this into speaking notes for a planning meeting.
3. Write this as 5 concise bullet points for a presentation slide.
4. Rewrite this in a more formal tone for senior leadership.
5. Write a concise weekly demand planning update based on this forecast run.
6. Turn this into a short WhatsApp-style update for an internal business stakeholder.
7. Rewrite this as a briefing note for tomorrow's management review.
8. Write this as a short narrative suitable for a boardroom slide.
9. Turn this into a one-minute verbal summary for a presenter.
10. Rewrite this as a decision memo with risks, implications, and actions.

---

## Confidence And Data Quality Prompts

1. Focus on what makes this forecast strong or weak from a confidence perspective.
2. Explain the main caveats decision-makers should know before acting on these numbers.
3. Highlight how much of this run depends on ML versus fallback methods and why that matters.
4. Summarize the biggest data quality warnings affecting forecast confidence.
5. Explain whether the current history depth is sufficient for reliable planning.
6. Focus on areas where sparse data may be distorting the narrative.
7. Write a forecast-confidence summary for management.
8. Explain what assumptions are carrying the most weight in this run.
9. Focus on the limits of the model and how users should interpret the results.
10. Summarize what additional data would improve confidence in future runs.

---

## Recommended Starter Prompts

If you are not sure what to type, start with one of these:

- Summarize this forecast run for senior management, focusing on top risks, reorder priorities, and forecast confidence.
- Write a buyer brief focused on urgent reorder actions and items needing manual review.
- Write a risk summary covering understock, overstock, fallback usage, and data quality caveats.
- Explain this brand's forecast in plain English, including the main actions the buyer should take.
- Rewrite this as a short leadership update suitable for a planning meeting.
